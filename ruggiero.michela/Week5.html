Week 5

3D Scanning and Printing

SCANNING

The exercises of the 5th week are the 3D scanning of a subject , and the 3D printing of an object modeled by us.

Tools:
3D Systems Sense Scanner
3D Print Wasp Delta

1 Steps: Scanning

We tried to scan body parts of our comrades, but I realized that was necessary to have a perfect control of the scanner, because it always lost the subject image.


So I proceeded as a first exercise by scanning an object.
I chose the arm of a mannequin because the goal of 3D printing exercise was to create women's accessories. I stayed on the topic using the model of the scanned arm.


To scan I used the 3D Systems Sense Scanner.
After scanning, I dsicovered that in the scanned model some closures were missing. I used the repairs tool to place them automatically.
- I saved the scanned image, created a file (sense-mano.ply), and opened it with Rhinoceros.



2 Steps: 3D PRINTING


The 3D printer at our disposal was the Wasp Delta 40x70. Print definition: 100micron, extruder: 0.4mm, filaments: 1.75.


The programs used were: Rhinoceros, Grasshopper, Cura, Photoshop.


The subjects I chose were two women's accessories: a necklace and a bracelet.


The 3D model was exported in .stl format by Grasshopper and imported into Cura to create the gcode for the machine.



In order to have more grip, the printer has been set with the floor at 100 °, and the extruder at 180°.


We have done a lot of tests because a every time the printed object lifted up from the floor.

The material used is yellow PLA 1,75mm.
