Assignment:
- Redraw the echo hello-world board,
- Add (at least) a button and LED (with current-limiting resistor)
- Check the design rules, and make it.

To redesign the board, we have downloaded the software Eagle, and some components library.


DESIGN THE CIRCUIT

I used Eagle to redesign the hello board.

Download-Eagle

CadSoft EAGLE is a software widely used by companies and by amateurs to draw PCB.

I followed the Providence tutorial electronics_design_eagle.html to design my circuit. I selected all the components that I needed from the libraries and I make the schematic connecting using the "net" tool.

I added one button and one LED to my hello board. In the board view I distributed the components attemping to create easier connections, and so drew the elettrical routes between them.


CREATING THE FILES

I hid all layers except the top one, and selected file> export> image. In the export image window I chose 500 dpi resolution and I checked the button Monochrome, creating the "traces.png" file. Then I edited it in Photoshop and I created a border by changing the canvas size, offsetting it by 20px for each side and painting it black. Then I painted the rest of the image white. In this way i made the "interior.png" file.


Tools:

- Roland MonoFab SRM20

Table size (LxP): 232.2 x 156.6 mm
Distance from collet tip to table: 232.2 x 156.6 mm

- DIA 0.0156" DIA 2FL SE AlTiN 1/64(engraving)
- 0.0312" DIA 2FL SE AlTiN 1/32(milling)

SETTING MACHINE:

- Open fabmodules.org
- Click on Input Format;
- Choose Image(.png) and Upload Image;
- Click on Output Format and choose "Roland Mill (.rml)";
- Machine Settings;
- Machine: SRM-20; Speed: 4; Jog: 1; Xmin: 0; Ymin: 0;
- CLick on Process and choose "PCB traces(1/64)" or "PCB outline(1/32)";
- Change offset to 5 (this is a personal setting);
- Hit Calculate;
- Save.
