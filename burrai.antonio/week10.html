Input devices

.

The first part of the weekly assignment is the design of a FabKit. FabKit has a lot of components, described in the BOM, so I need half an hour to collect them all using the method described by the previous picture.

The most complex component is surely the ATMEGA328, that is very small and very difficult to solder.

It takes one hour or more to solder all the components, briefly resumed in the following: Components: Atmega 328P, Switch Button, FTDI Communication, 8 MHz external resonator, 3 Capacitors (2 : 0.1uf and 1: 1uf), 2 Resistor (10kΩ and 499kΩ) and 1 LED.

After this, I start studying the ATMEGA328 pinout, it has been quite complex, but at the end, in order to write the bootloader, I understand I need the following pins: MISO,MOSI, SCK, RESET, VCC and GND.

A good help for finding the correct usage has been Eagle, who enlight every trace from the beginning to the end. I take note of the pins I’m going to use for the project.

At the moment I’m experiencing different problem trying to write the bootloader. I’m looking for the problem, maybe a soldering problem or maybe the high temperature destroyed or damaged the ATMEGA328. This section will be updated as soon as possible.
