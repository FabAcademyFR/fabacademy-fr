Input devices

.

The first part of the weekly assignment is the design of a FabKit and an input device. FabKit, who has been designed for first, has a lot of components, described in the BOM, so I need half an hour to collect them all using the method described by the previous picture.

Apart from the component, I drilled the board using the Roland machine. Three different tasks have been performed: traces (1/64), drill and cut (1/32).

The most complex component is surely the ATMEGA328, that is very small and very difficult to solder. The other components are briefly resumed in the following: Components: Atmega 328P, Switch Button, FTDI Communication, 8 MHz external resonator, 3 Capacitors (2 : 0.1uf and 1: 1uf), 2 Resistor (10kΩ and 499kΩ) and 1 LED.

As it can be seen, FabKit is really very very small !!!!

After this, I start studying the ATMEGA328 pinout, it has been quite complex, but at the end, in order to write the BOOTLOADER, I understand I need the following pins: MISO,MOSI, SCK, RESET, VCC and GND.

A good help for finding the correct usage has been Eagle, who ENLIGHT EVERY TRACE from the beginning to the end. I take note of the pins I’m going to use for the project.

At the beginning I experienced different problem trying to write the bootloader. I’ve tried to use Arduino as ISP, just to make a test, but it was a procedure very full of problem, so I give up.

I decide to use an USB programmer, so I powered the FabKit using the FTDI cable. Then I’ve built a cable used to connect the programmer to the FabKit.

During the above mentioned connection, Windows required lots of drivers in order to make usable even the cable !!!

I change the boards list in the Arduino folder and the operation (thanks God!) went good at first time ! I’m very proud of my FabKit.

Despite of my final project it’s a fish, I’ve used a light sensor, considering that sun light is RELATED TO THE THE DEPTH of the water at which the fish is swimming. The deeper you go, the darker it is.

As weel as a submarine, if the depth is excessive and the pressure is too high, several damages can occur to the hull. So the purpose of the input device is to send a constant signal to the processor in order to measure indirectly the depth of water.

This is the folder of my input device milling files: PNG and RML files.

At the end of the soldering I burn the resistor and destroy also a portion of the traces (blue arrow zone) !!! I didn’t have the time to go back to the FabLab, so, just because it was an emergency I place one of the header connector in an unused part of the traces (green arrow) and I’ve also used a classic resistor .
